Recently Adopted

These are just a handful of successful adoption stories from a list of many.
Check them out! Are you next?

Iggy and Ben

Iggy (who's name was Carson at the shelter) was the only one of his litter that had not been adopted. When we came to visit the shelter, intent on adopting orange kittens, the first kitten we noticed was Iggy who was in a cage with another kitten family. While they all slept and ate, he pranced at the front of the pen trying to get our attention. He was scruffy and black and not at all what we had in mind. I asked to hold him and immediately fell in love with him. He is the sweetest boy. My love!

Fred and The Williams Family

Fred came in as a stray, completely emaciated. He was old, suffering from frostbite on his ear, poor vision and hearing loss. He sat in the shelter for months with 0 adoption interest. My family and I seen his picture online, and decided to go meet him. (His rescue sister needed a friend!). He was nervous, but we fell completely in love and took him home the very next day. The rest is history. He is my best friend (although my kids will argue he's theirs!)

Peppy and Genna

Peppy is beyond special, he is probably the most loyal and well behaved dog I have ever had. We've learned through Peppy that dogs really can express emotion especially when it comes to how grateful they are for you. From what we have been told but the shelter's Peppy had a very rough beginning and was found in the woods and had to be trapped to be helped. He has come such a long way from the partially bold very timid dog that we adopted in May. He truly has an amazing personality!

Sophie and Kimmy

Sophie has a happy "let's play" smile. People always ask if they can pet her. It must be that welcoming smile. We found Sophie at the SPCA during the Christmas season. She is a great hiking companion and despite her size wants to be a lap dog
